About Climate Change

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities have been the primary driver of an unprecedented rate of warming over the past century.

The Greenhouse Effect

The main cause of climate change is the greenhouse effect. Certain gases in the Earth's atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4), trap heat from the sun, preventing it from escaping back into space. This leads to a warming of the planet. Human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes increase the concentration of these greenhouse gases.

Effects of Climate Change

The effects of climate change are far-reaching and varied, including:

  • Rising sea levels due to melting polar ice caps and glaciers
  • More frequent and severe weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves
  • Disruption of ecosystems and loss of biodiversity
  • Impacts on agriculture and food security
  • Health risks due to heat stress, waterborne diseases, and poor air quality

What Can Be Done?

Addressing climate change requires collective action at all levels, including:

  • Reducing greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning to renewable energy sources like solar and wind
  • Improving energy efficiency in homes, buildings, and transportation
  • Protecting and restoring forests, wetlands, and other natural carbon sinks
  • Supporting policies and initiatives that promote sustainable practices
  • Educating and empowering individuals to make environmentally-friendly choices
